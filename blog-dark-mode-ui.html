Dark Mode UI: Best Practices

Jan 28, 2026 • 4 min read • By Maria Design

Dark mode isn't just a trend; it's a user expectation. But implementing a true dark mode is more nuanced than just inverting colors. It requires careful consideration of contrast, depth, and color saturation.

1. Avoid Pure Black

Using pure black (#000000) for backgrounds can cause eye strain and "smearing" on OLED screens when scrolling. Instead, use a very dark grey (like #121212). This softens the contrast and allows for utilizing shadows to create depth.

2. Desaturate Your Colors

Bright, saturated colors that look great on white backgrounds can vibrate against dark backgrounds, making text hard to read. When designing for dark mode, use lighter, desaturated pastels or adjust the opacity of your primary colors.

3. Use Elevation for Hierarchy

In light mode, we often use shadows to show elevation. In dark mode, shadows are less visible. Instead, use lighter shades of grey to indicate elevation. The "closer" an element is to the user (like a modal or card), the lighter its background color should be.

Conclusion

Great dark mode design is about empathy—understanding the user's environment and ensuring readability. By avoiding pure black and handling color with care, you can create an experience that feels premium and polished.
